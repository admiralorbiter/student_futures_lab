# Teacher Facilitation Guide

*5-class plan for the Hickman Mills Pathway Advisory Team project*

Use this guide to facilitate the inquiry and prepare for the app build without giving away the adult synthesis too early.

---

## Project purpose

- Students are acting as a small advisory team, not as junior spreadsheet analysts.
- The main product is a recommendation on which pathways look strongest and most reachable for Hickman Mills students.
- The secondary product is an individual reflection that stress-tests each student's own plan against the evidence.
- The process should also generate usable student insight for PREP-KC.

## What is in this material set?

| Document | How it is used |
|----------|---------------|
| Student Core Inquiry Packet | Main working packet across all five classes. Houses the full inquiry arc and final recommendation. |
| Student Glossary and How to Read the Data | Plain-language definitions, question prompts, and data cautions. |
| Student Daily Evidence Catcher | One structured note page per class period. Useful for both reflection and data capture. |
| Student Final Presentation Planner | Slide storyboard, speaker roles, sentence starters, and rehearsal checks. |
| PREP-KC Student Insight Capture Sheet | Teacher-facing note sheet for capturing useful student voice and patterns for PREP-KC. |
| App Specification | Companion document for building the guided app and saving student outputs. |

## Non-negotiable facilitation choices

- Treat the class as a small advisory studio. The goal is interpretation and recommendation, not a giant data-cleaning exercise.
- Do not reveal the adult synthesis first. Let students make an independent read before they see how it compares to your own conclusions.
- Keep pathway families as the main unit of comparison before showing lots of institution detail.
- Build in individual thinking before group discussion so one strong voice does not dominate the room.
- Use Hickman Mills as the reality filter. "Strong in Kansas City" is not automatically the same as "reachable from here."
- Make students say what evidence led them to each conclusion. Do not let them jump from preference to recommendation without showing their reasoning.

---

## 5-class scope and sequence

| Class | Main focus | What students produce | What you collect |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Project frame + personal criteria + first opportunity read | Ranked criteria, initial top 2 pathways, first opportunity buckets | Criteria rankings, initial pathway hypotheses, terms students need clarified |
| 2 | Finish opportunity read + Hickman Mills filter | Top 3 pathways, barrier notes, support notes | What moved up/down, common barriers, first support ideas |
| 3 | Launch points + bridge roles | Realistic start-point matches for top pathways | Which launch points felt believable, what first steps seem realistic |
| 4 | Personal reality check + start group synthesis | Completed individual reflection + early recommendation claims | How student plans shifted, what supports feel most important |
| 5 | Finalize recommendation + build presentation | Slide storyboard, final recommendation, rehearsal notes | Final top 2, caution pathway, supports, student language that resonates |

### If time gets compressed

- Merge Class 3 and Class 4 by making launch points and the personal reality check happen in the same period.
- Protect Class 1 and Class 2. If students do not get a clean opportunity read and a clean Hickman Mills filter, the later recommendation will be weaker.

---

## Class 1 — Frame the advisory role and start the first read

Students should understand the main question, surface what matters to them personally, and make a first pathway judgment based only on opportunity signals.

### Show in class / app

- Project question and the seven pathways
- Step 1 of the packet
- App Screen 1 only: pathway-family evidence such as wage, scale, projected demand, county fit, and example roles

### Do not show yet

- Institution details
- Adult pathway priorities
- Large employer maps
- The internal strategy report

### Teacher moves

- Start with: "You are not being asked to tell PREP-KC what is coolest. You are being asked to decide what seems strongest and most reachable for students like you."
- Have each student complete Step 1 silently before group discussion.
- Ask for initial top 2 pathways before they see much data so you can compare early hunches with later evidence.
- Push them to bucket all seven pathways, even when they feel uncertain.

### Outputs to collect

- Step 1 rankings
- Initial pathway buckets
- Day 1 evidence catcher

### Useful prompts

- What makes a pathway feel worth it to you?
- What are you noticing when you look only at pathway evidence?
- Which pathways look strong on paper even before we think about colleges?
- Where are you already tempted to jump ahead?

---

## Class 2 — Apply the Hickman Mills lens

Students should revise their first ranking by naming what changes once local reachability, transportation, access barriers, and bridge roles are considered.

### Show in class / app

- Finish any remaining Screen 1 work
- App Screen 2: Hickman Mills lens notes, county role, transportation flags, bridge-role examples, local access notes

### Do not show yet

- Every institution at once
- Full employer lists
- Adult conclusions about what PREP-KC should do

### Teacher moves

- Tell students to focus only on their top 3 pathways from Step 2.
- Ask them to explain what stayed strong, not only what became harder.
- Make them distinguish barriers from supports. A barrier is what gets in the way; a support is what could make the pathway usable.
- If the group stays abstract, pull them back to: "Would this still feel real from Hickman Mills?"

### Outputs to collect

- Revised top pathways
- Barrier list
- Support list
- Day 2 evidence catcher

### Useful prompts

- What changes once you think about Hickman Mills students specifically?
- What still looks strong even after you add the local lens?
- What could get in the way — and what would help?
- Which pathway moved up or down, and why?

---

## Class 3 — Identify realistic launch points and believable first steps

Students should match their strongest pathways to realistic starting places and bridge roles rather than defaulting to prestige or destination names.

### Show in class / app

- App Screen 3: launch points, institution archetypes, bridge roles, and first-step notes
- Step 4 of the packet

### Do not show yet

- Huge institution lists
- Detailed program catalogs that overwhelm the decision
- Untranslated technical data fields

### Teacher moves

- Keep the question framed as "Where could someone realistically start?"
- When students fixate on brand-name colleges, ask what the first believable step would be.
- Have them justify launch points in plain language: why this start, for which student, and with what watch-out?
- Connect bridge roles to launch points so the story feels real.

### Outputs to collect

- Top pathway-to-launch-point matches
- Bridge-role notes
- Day 3 evidence catcher

### Useful prompts

- Which start point feels realistic, not just impressive?
- What first role or early step makes this pathway more believable?
- What questions do you still have about how students would get from school to work or training?

---

## Class 4 — Personal reality check and early synthesis

Students should connect the evidence to their own plans and then bring that learning back into the group recommendation.

### Show in class / app

- App Screen 4: My Pathway Reality Check
- Personal reflection prompts
- If ready, an early version of Screen 5

### Do not show yet

- Labels or scores that make students feel judged
- A teacher lecture about the "right" answer

### Teacher moves

- Give students real individual work time first.
- Make it clear that personal reflection is not about forcing everyone into the same recommendation.
- After reflection, ask what the data confirmed, complicated, or changed for them personally.
- Then bring that insight back to the group: what does this mean for Hickman Mills students more broadly?

### Outputs to collect

- Completed personal reflection
- Early group recommendation claims
- Day 4 evidence catcher

### Useful prompts

- What did the data strengthen for you?
- What did it complicate?
- What first step now feels most realistic for you?
- What support would make the biggest difference?

---

## Class 5 — Finalize recommendation and build the presentation

Students should turn their evidence into a clear, actionable recommendation and prepare to defend it in a short presentation.

### Show in class / app

- App Screen 5: recommendation builder and export
- Student Presentation Planner
- Teacher challenge questions for rehearsal

### Do not show yet

- Last-minute new data that changes the whole frame
- Too many preset answers in the builder

### Teacher moves

- Require evidence in every section of the final recommendation.
- Have students practice answering why they chose their top 2 pathways and why one pathway deserves caution.
- Push them to make their message to PREP-KC sound usable and concrete.
- Use the rehearsal to surface where they still need sharper evidence or language.

### Outputs to collect

- Final recommendation table
- Presentation storyboard
- Day 5 evidence catcher

### Useful prompts

- What is the clearest claim you want the audience to remember?
- What evidence makes your recommendation more than a personal opinion?
- What support would make the biggest difference?
- What is one honest uncertainty you still want to name?

---

## Prompt bank for common student moves

| When students … | Try asking … |
|----------------|-------------|
| Focus only on openings or scale | Does this pathway also have wages, a believable first step, and room to move up? |
| Jump straight to colleges | What do you know about the pathway itself before you decide where someone should start? |
| Keep talking about Kansas City overall | Would the answer change if you were thinking specifically about Hickman Mills students? |
| Assume high pay automatically means best | Is it reachable? What would students need in order to benefit from it? |
| Default to a low-pay job | Is that a true pathway recommendation, or just a stepping-stone job with no visible ladder? |
| Get stuck on a niche or off-scope question | Is that something we should answer now, or something we should park for later research? |
| Use a technical label without explaining it | Can you translate that field into plain English before you use it as evidence? |

---

## Questions to honor — but park for later

- Very narrow field or school questions that fall outside the seven pathways.
- Long-term outcomes we do not yet have clean data for, such as job satisfaction, promotion timing, or multi-year retention.
- Special-topic questions such as military benefits or animal-care programs that are important, but outside the main project scope.
- Anything that requires a full employer analysis before the employer layer is actually ready.

---

## Backup plan if the app is not ready

- Use the core packet as the main backbone.
- Print or project small pathway tables instead of a live explorer.
- Reveal information in the same sequence as the planned app: opportunity first, then Hickman Mills lens, then launch points, then personal reflection, then recommendation.
- Do not wait for a perfect app. A guided sequence matters more than a flashy interface.

---

## What a strong final presentation includes

| Criterion | What strong work sounds like |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| Clear claim | The team makes a recommendation instead of only describing data. |
| Evidence use | Students point to specific evidence rather than making unsupported guesses. |
| Local lens | The team explains how Hickman Mills changes or sharpens the answer. |
| Realistic starts | Launch points and bridge roles feel believable, not just impressive. |
| Actionable support | The team can say what PREP-KC could do to make a pathway more reachable. |
| Honesty | The team names at least one uncertainty or caution rather than pretending the data is perfect. |
