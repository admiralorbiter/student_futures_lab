# Final Presentation Planner

*Storyboard, speaking roles, and rehearsal checklist*

Use this once your team starts turning evidence into a recommendation.

---

## Your presentation should answer one question

**Which pathways look strongest for Hickman Mills students when you consider opportunity, reachability, and support needs?**

The goal is not only to name strong pathways. The goal is also to explain what would make those pathways believable, reachable, and worth pursuing for students like you.

---

## One team, four voices

| Suggested role | Main job during prep | Who will do it? |
|---------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Evidence lead** | Makes sure the team can point to actual pathway evidence and explain what looked strong on paper. | |
| **Hickman Mills lens lead** | Tracks what changed once transportation, county fit, local access, and support needs were considered. | |
| **Start-here lead** | Explains launch points, bridge roles, and why the team chose realistic first steps. | |
| **Recommendation lead** | Helps the team craft its final message to PREP-KC and leads the closing recommendation. | |

You can keep these roles or rotate them. The main goal is to make sure every student has a visible contribution.

---

## Strong evidence checklist

- Use at least 5 separate pieces of evidence across the whole presentation.
- Do not repeat the same fact on every slide.
- Make sure at least one piece of evidence comes from the Hickman Mills lens and at least one comes from the launch-point view.
- If your team disagreed somewhere, name that honestly instead of pretending there was no debate.

---

## Message and sentence starters

| Use this when you need a stronger sentence | Try saying … |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------|
| Making a claim | "We think PREP-KC should prioritize ___ because the evidence showed ___." |
| Adding the Hickman Mills lens | "That answer changed once we thought about transportation, local access, and supports because ___." |
| Explaining a launch point | "We chose ___ as a realistic launch point because it feels like a believable first step, not just a strong name." |
| Naming a caution | "We are more cautious about ___ because it looks strong in some ways but harder to reach for students like us." |
| Ending with action | "The support that would make the biggest difference is ___ because ___." |
